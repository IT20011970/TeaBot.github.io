About the Project

Automated Fertilizing Robot for Tea Plantations

Tea cultivation is a vital industry, and optimizing the farming process is crucial for ensuring high-quality yield. Introducing TeaFertico, an innovative solution revolutionizing tea plantation management through automation. TeaFertico is an IoT-based automated fertilizing robot designed specifically for tea farmers, aiming to enhance productivity, reduce labor, and promote sustainable agricultural practices.

  • Research Gap
  • Problem
  • Objectives

The agricultural sector, particularly tea plantations, has seen significant advancements in automation technology. Traditional farming practices are being revolutionized through automation, offering farmers efficient ways to manage their crops. In this context, the development of an Automated Fertilizing Robot tailored for tea plantations represents a crucial step forward.

In the field of automated fertilizing robots for tea plantations, substantial research gaps persist. Existing studies lack a comprehensive focus on robots specifically designed for tea cultivation, leading to an inadequate understanding of their efficacy in optimizing fertilizer application. Moreover, there is a noticeable absence of standardized evaluation methods, hindering accurate assessments of these robots' performance in real tea plantation environments. . Additionally, while other domains have extensively studied the collaborative and playful aspects of human-robot interaction, such research is lacking in the context of tea plantation automation. Furthermore, insufficient attention has been given to assessing the functionality of automated fertilizing robots concerning diverse soil types, weather conditions, and tea plant varieties. Addressing these research gaps is essential to unlock the full potential of automated fertilizing robots, enabling the development of practical, efficient, and user-friendly solutions tailored to the specific challenges of tea cultivation.

In the realm of agricultural technology, a pressing research problem revolves around the evolving use of digital devices by farmers. While technology is becoming ubiquitous, particularly in tea plantations, there is a crucial need to bridge the gap between digital interfaces and effective agricultural practices. Presently, farmers often resort to smartphones for various tasks, including agricultural management. However, the complexity of tea plantation processes demands a more intuitive approach. Traditional touchscreens fall short in providing the necessary hands-on experience required for tasks like fertilizing. To enhance the efficiency and learning curve for farmers, there is a compelling requirement for interfaces specifically tailored for tea cultivation. Finding innovative solutions that combine practical elements with agricultural technology is imperative for optimizing tea plantation practices and ensuring a sustainable and productive future for farmers.
